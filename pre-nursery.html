Pre‑Nursery Program

3.5 – 4.5 years | Foundations in literacy and numeracy

About Our Pre‑Nursery Program

Our Pre-Nursery Program establishes strong foundations in literacy and numeracy through hands-on Montessori materials. Designed for children aged 3.5 to 4.5 years, this program prepares children for formal academic learning while maintaining their natural love for discovery.

Using the proven Montessori method, children engage with specially designed materials that make abstract concepts concrete, fostering deep understanding and genuine enthusiasm for learning.

Program Highlights

Phonics & Letters

Systematic introduction to letter sounds, phonetic awareness, and early reading skills through multi-sensory Montessori language materials.

Number Sense

Mathematical concepts introduced through concrete materials, developing number recognition, counting, and basic arithmetic understanding.

Fine Motor Skills

Precise hand movements developed through practical life activities, writing preparation, and manipulative materials.

Curriculum Areas

Language Arts

  • Sandpaper letters for tactile letter learning
  • Moveable alphabet for word building
  • Phonetic reading and writing exercises
  • Vocabulary enrichment activities
  • Story comprehension and retelling

Mathematics

  • Number rods and golden beads for quantity
  • Counting and number recognition 1-100
  • Introduction to addition and subtraction
  • Geometric shapes and patterns
  • Measurement and comparison concepts

Sensorial

  • Pink tower and brown stair for dimension
  • Color tablets for visual discrimination
  • Sound cylinders for auditory development
  • Fabric boxes for tactile exploration
  • Geometric solids for 3D understanding

Practical Life

  • Care of self and environment
  • Food preparation and serving
  • Grace and courtesy lessons
  • Fine motor skill development
  • Independence and responsibility

Learning Outcomes

Academic Readiness

  • Phonetic reading and basic writing skills
  • Number concepts and mathematical thinking
  • Problem-solving and logical reasoning
  • Concentration and task completion

Motor Development

  • Refined fine motor control for writing
  • Hand-eye coordination and precision
  • Gross motor skills and body awareness
  • Spatial orientation and movement control

Social-Emotional Growth

  • Self-regulation and emotional awareness
  • Empathy and consideration for others
  • Leadership and helping behaviors
  • Confidence in learning and exploration

Independence & Life Skills

  • Self-care and personal responsibility
  • Environmental care and stewardship
  • Time management and routine following
  • Decision-making and choice ownership
