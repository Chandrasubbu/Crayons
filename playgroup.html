Playgroup Program

2.5 – 3.5 years | Play-based learning for independence and creativity

About Our Playgroup Program

Our Playgroup Program emphasizes play-based learning that builds independence, creativity, and collaboration. Designed for children aged 2.5 to 3.5 years, this program creates a bridge between toddlerhood and more structured learning environments.

Through engaging activities and guided exploration, children develop critical thinking skills, emotional intelligence, and social competence while maintaining the joy and wonder of childhood learning.

Program Highlights

Art & Craft

Creative expression through painting, drawing, collage, and hands-on craft projects that develop fine motor skills and artistic appreciation.

Stories & Rhyme

Interactive storytelling, nursery rhymes, and dramatic play that enhance language development, imagination, and listening skills.

Group Activities

Collaborative games, circle time, and team projects that foster social skills, cooperation, and emotional development.

Learning Outcomes

Creative Development

  • Artistic expression through various mediums
  • Imagination and creative thinking skills
  • Appreciation for music, art, and literature
  • Development of personal artistic style

Social Skills

  • Cooperation and teamwork abilities
  • Sharing and turn-taking skills
  • Empathy and understanding of others
  • Conflict resolution and problem-solving

Language & Communication

  • Expanded vocabulary and verbal expression
  • Listening and comprehension skills
  • Storytelling and narrative abilities
  • Pre-reading and pre-writing foundations

Independence & Confidence

  • Self-directed learning and choice-making
  • Personal responsibility and self-care
  • Confidence in new situations
  • Initiative and leadership qualities

Special Activities

Drama & Role Play

Dress-up corner and dramatic play activities that encourage imagination, language development, and social interaction through pretend scenarios.

Nature Exploration

Garden activities, nature walks, and environmental awareness programs that connect children with the natural world and foster scientific curiosity.

Music & Movement

Singing, dancing, and rhythm activities that develop musical appreciation, coordination, and provide joyful expression opportunities.

Problem Solving Games

Age-appropriate puzzles, building blocks, and logic games that enhance cognitive development and critical thinking skills.
